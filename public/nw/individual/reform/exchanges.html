What is the Health Insurance "Exchange"?

A health insurance exchange is a government regulated insurance market place for individuals and small group employers to shop for health plans. Some states such as Oregon will have a State run market place, while others have elected a federally facilitated marketplace.

The Exchange In Oregon

The Oregon Health Exchange which is called “Cover Oregon” will serve as a one stop resource that will allow for a true “apples to apples” comparison of plans, as all plans must be alike within the Exchange.

Beginning in October of 2013 the Oregon Exchange will begin the open enrollment process. Plans that are then purchased through Cover Oregon will begin on January 1st, 2014.

Both individuals and small group employer’s size 2-50 will have access to see if they will qualify for financial assistance. Individuals and families may receive financial help paying for premiums in the form of tax credits, while small employers with fewer than 25 full time employees paying an average wage of less than $50,000 per year may qualify for a tax credit worth up to 50% of premium costs.
